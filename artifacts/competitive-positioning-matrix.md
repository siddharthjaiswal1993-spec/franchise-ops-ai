## Dimension Definitions

**Franchise-Native Depth:** Does the platform natively understand the franchise relationship structure — FDD management, brand-to-location hierarchy, HQ-franchisee-field-consultant roles, royalty management, multi-brand normalization, and franchise lifecycle from development through operations?

**Operational Intelligence Maturity:** Does the platform provide proactive, predictive operational insights? Specifically: location health scoring, anomaly detection, risk prediction, AI-powered field consulting recommendations, and outcome verification?

**Workflow Coverage:** How broad is the platform's coverage of franchise operational workflows? Does it cover franchise development, pre-opening, operations, training, compliance, field operations, royalty, and marketing? Or is it focused on a subset of the lifecycle?

**AI Readiness:** What is the platform's current AI capability level, and how architected is the platform for AI expansion? Includes current AI features, integration infrastructure for AI data inputs, and architectural readiness for intelligence layer addition.

**Enterprise Scalability:** Can the platform serve large, complex franchise systems (500+ locations) with multi-brand complexity, enterprise security requirements, role-based data access at scale, and integration with enterprise systems (SAP, Oracle, Workday)?

## Strategic Implications

**FranConnect's current advantage:** The only platform with Franchise-Native Depth = 5 and Workflow Coverage = 5 simultaneously. This is a structural advantage that takes years to replicate. No competitor can claim both the franchise-native data model depth and the full lifecycle workflow coverage that FranConnect has built.

**FranConnect's primary gap:** Operational Intelligence Maturity (currently 3) and AI Readiness (currently 3). These are the categories that define the next competitive era. Moving from 3 to 5 on both dimensions — while maintaining the franchise-native depth advantage — is the product investment imperative.

**ServiceTitan's threat:** ServiceTitan's AI investment and enterprise scalability scores are higher than FranConnect's AI readiness score. If ServiceTitan expands its franchise-native capabilities (royalty, FDD, field consulting), it becomes a genuine platform threat in home services franchise verticals. This competitive risk should be monitored.

**Delightree's limitation:** Delightree's Franchise-Native Depth (2) and Enterprise Scalability (2) scores cap its addressable market. It is a strong competitor for the 50-200 location mobile-first mid-market segment but is not currently competitive for enterprise or PE-backed brands that need franchise lifecycle depth and AI intelligence.

**Where to compete aggressively:** Field consulting intelligence (FranConnect has no current competitor), executive intelligence for PE-backed brands (no current competitor), and enterprise franchise brands where franchise-native depth is a hard requirement.
