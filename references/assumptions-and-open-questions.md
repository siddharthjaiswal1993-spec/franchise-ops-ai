# Assumptions and Open Questions

> 15 key assumptions made in this analysis and 15 open questions to validate with FranConnect leadership, organized by category.

---

## Assumptions

### Market Assumptions

**A1.** The franchise operations software market is approaching a category transition from workflow digitization to operational intelligence, and this transition will be substantially complete within 5-7 years.

**A2.** PE-backed franchise brands and multi-unit operators represent a disproportionate share of the high-value, intelligence-tier ICP — both in willingness to pay and in ROI articulation fluency.

**A3.** The corrective action closure rate problem is a near-universal pain point across franchise systems, with industry average rates in the 55-65% range. This assumption is based on industry practitioner conversations and is not from published research.

**A4.** Field consultants in mid-market franchise systems spend 30-40% of their time on administrative tasks (visit preparation, note writing, corrective action tracking). This estimate is directionally validated but not precisely measured across a large sample.

**A5.** The competitive window for defining the franchise operational intelligence category is 18-24 months before AI-native entrants or Delightree expand upmarket with credible intelligence capabilities.

### Product Assumptions

**A6.** The Location Health Score requires a minimum of 3 integrated data sources (LMS + audit + one additional) to produce a reliable model. Below this threshold, prediction accuracy is insufficient for operational trust.

**A7.** Integration setup (LMS, audit, basic POS) for an AI Field Consultant pilot can be completed within 2 weeks at a customer with standard technical infrastructure.

**A8.** Voice-to-structured notes with 80%+ tag accuracy is achievable with current commercial speech-to-text APIs combined with a custom franchise operations tagging taxonomy.

**A9.** Field consultants will achieve measurable adoption of AI pre-visit briefs within 3-4 weeks of introduction, given adequate change management and champion identification.

**A10.** The Location Health Score's predictive accuracy (80%+ of At Risk states followed by measurable decline if untreated) is achievable after 12+ months of multi-source data accumulation and model training.

### GTM Assumptions

**A11.** A funded pilot pricing of $15,000-$25,000 for an 8-week AI Field Consultant pilot is within the budget approval authority of a Director of Field Operations or VP of Operations at mid-market franchise brands.

**A12.** Pilot-to-contract conversion rates of 70%+ are achievable with an 8-week AI Field Consultant pilot that hits the target ROI metrics (8+ hours/week recaptured per FC, corrective action closure rate improvement).

**A13.** The franchise consultant and broker channel is an accessible pipeline source for mid-market franchise brands, and referral economics of 10-15% first-year fee are sufficient to motivate partner engagement.

**A14.** PE operating partners are accessible through an invitation-only roundtable program and represent a viable channel for multi-portfolio franchise brand deployments.

**A15.** The category name "franchise operational intelligence" is available and differentiating — it has not yet been claimed by a competitor as a primary category positioning.

## Open Questions to Validate with FranConnect Leadership

### Market Questions

**Q1.** How is FranConnect currently measuring its competitive position against Delightree — is Delightree appearing in competitive deal evaluations, or are they primarily serving different market segments?

**Q2.** What is the current distribution of FranConnect's customer base by size segment — what percentage of ARR comes from mid-market (50-500 locations), enterprise (500+), and emerging (<50 locations) franchise brands?

**Q3.** How many of FranConnect's current enterprise and mid-market customers are PE-backed, and has the team identified a specific growth strategy for the PE operating partner channel?

**Q4.** What franchise verticals beyond QSR represent the strongest growth opportunity for the intelligence tier — home services, fitness, personal care, or others?

**Q5.** What does the customer interview data say about the most acute operational pain — is field consulting consistently the highest-pain problem, or does compliance or franchisee health score surface as more urgent in some segments?

### Product Questions

**Q6.** Where is the Location Health Score in the product roadmap — is it in design, development, beta, or production? And what is the primary technical constraint on acceleration?

**Q7.** What is the current average number of API integrations per active FranConnect customer, and which source systems (POS, HRIS, review platforms) have the lowest current adoption among the customer base?

**Q8.** Has the team validated the AI pre-visit brief concept with field consultants in any existing customers? What was the qualitative feedback on what they would find useful vs. redundant?

**Q9.** What is the current trust architecture for AI features in the product — is there a source citation system, a confidence scoring model, or human approval gate infrastructure in place today?

**Q10.** What is the biggest technical debt challenge for building the intelligence layer on top of the current platform architecture?

### AI Questions

**Q11.** What is FranConnect's current AI capability stack — is the team building models in-house, using third-party AI platforms (OpenAI, AWS, GCP), or a combination?

**Q12.** Does the platform currently collect structured, normalized data that could feed a health score model, or is significant data normalization work required before AI model development can begin?

**Q13.** How is the product team thinking about the franchise-specific AI governance requirements — has there been customer input on what source citations, confidence scoring, and human approval requirements look like for enterprise compliance buyers?

### GTM Questions

**Q14.** What is the current deal velocity for mid-market franchise brands — how long is the average sales cycle from first contact to signed contract, and what are the most common friction points in the evaluation process?

**Q15.** Does FranConnect have a structured pilot program today, or is the typical engagement a full platform sale? Is there openness to a pilot-first GTM motion as a pipeline development tool?
