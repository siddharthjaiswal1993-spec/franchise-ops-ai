# Persona Mapping: Franchise Operations Platform

> Detailed persona profiles for 13 stakeholders across the franchise operations ecosystem.

---

## Persona 1: CEO / Founder

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | 1. Grow the franchise system at the pace the operating model can support. 2. Maintain brand consistency and protect brand equity as the system scales. 3. Demonstrate operational performance metrics that support fundraising, PE exit, or strategic sale valuation. |
| **Pain Points** | 1. No real-time portfolio health view — operational crises are discovered through field reports or franchisee complaints, not early warning systems. 2. Difficulty articulating to investors or a board how operational risk is being managed across 200+ independently owned locations. 3. Time consumed by operational firefighting rather than strategic growth work. |
| **Metrics Owned** | AUV trend, system-wide revenue, franchisee satisfaction score, brand compliance rate, new unit opening velocity |
| **Buying Triggers** | 1. Preparing for PE fundraise or strategic sale requiring clean operational story. 2. Public audit failure, brand incident, or franchisee council pressure creating operational urgency. |
| **Objections** | 1. "We already have FranConnect — why do we need something more?" 2. "Our franchisees won't adopt another platform." |
| **Relevant Modules** | Executive Command Center, Location Health Score, Risk and Alerts Engine |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Real-time portfolio health visibility for CEOs who manage distributed execution risk across hundreds of independently owned locations." |

---

## Persona 2: COO / Head of Operations

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | 1. Maintain operational consistency across the full location portfolio. 2. Improve field consultant efficiency and measurable impact. 3. Build a scalable operations infrastructure that supports franchise system growth without proportional headcount growth. |
| **Pain Points** | 1. Field consulting team is under-tooled — spending 30-40% of time on admin, with no consistent measure of visit quality or impact. 2. No unified location health model makes resource allocation decisions subjective and politically complex. 3. Corrective action closure rates are below 65% and there is no mechanism to improve them systematically. |
| **Metrics Owned** | Corrective action closure rate, field visit frequency vs. standard, audit pass rate, operational consistency index |
| **Buying Triggers** | 1. New COO hire (90-day operational assessment creates change mandate). 2. Field consultant team growing faster than current tools can support. |
| **Objections** | 1. "Our field consultants won't change how they work." 2. "We've tried to improve corrective action tracking before and it didn't stick." |
| **Relevant Modules** | Field Consultant Workspace, Location Health Score, Closed-Loop Actions, Executive Command Center |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Operational intelligence that tells you which locations need attention this week — not which ones needed attention last month." |

---

## Persona 3: VP of Franchise Operations

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | 1. Standardize operational processes and monitoring across an expanding location portfolio. 2. Build a data-driven operations function that can demonstrate ROI to the COO and CEO. 3. Reduce the time spent on manual data compilation and reactive firefighting. |
| **Pain Points** | 1. Operational data lives in 4-6 disconnected systems with no unified view. 2. Monthly operations reviews require days of manual data aggregation. 3. No objective framework for prioritizing field consultant attention across the portfolio. |
| **Metrics Owned** | Operations consistency score, field consultant portfolio balance, time-to-corrective-action-closure, visit completion rate vs. schedule |
| **Buying Triggers** | 1. New VP hire looking to make immediate operational impact. 2. Franchise system crossing 100 locations where current tools break. |
| **Objections** | 1. "Integration with our current systems will take months." 2. "I need to validate ROI before I can get budget approval." |
| **Relevant Modules** | Field Consultant Workspace, Location Health Score, Risk and Alerts Engine, Benchmarking |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "A single operational view of every location in your system — updated continuously, not compiled manually every month." |

## Persona 4: Director of Field Operations

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | 1. Maximize the operational impact of each field consultant visit. 2. Ensure corrective actions from field visits are actually completed. 3. Demonstrate the ROI of the field consulting function to leadership. |
| **Pain Points** | 1. Field consultants spend too much time on visit preparation, note-writing, and follow-up emails — not enough time coaching. 2. No standardized framework for what a high-quality field visit looks like. 3. No measurement of whether field visits actually improved location performance. |
| **Metrics Owned** | Visit completion rate, pre-visit brief quality score, corrective action closure rate post-visit, location health score change following visits |
| **Buying Triggers** | 1. Field consultant team expansion creating need for standardization. 2. COO requesting proof of field consulting ROI. |
| **Objections** | 1. "My consultants are experienced — they don't need AI telling them how to do their jobs." 2. "We already have a visit form and process." |
| **Relevant Modules** | Field Consultant Workspace, Visit Intelligence, Closed-Loop Actions, AI Field Consultant |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Pre-visit briefs generated in seconds. Structured note capture in the field. Corrective action plans auto-drafted before the consultant leaves the parking lot." |

## Persona 5: Director of Training

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | 1. Maximize training completion rates and knowledge retention across all locations. 2. Demonstrate that training investment is linked to operational performance improvement. 3. Reduce the burden of manual training tracking and reporting. |
| **Pain Points** | 1. Ongoing training completion rates drop sharply after initial onboarding — reminder emails are not effective. 2. No connection between training completion data and operational performance data — cannot prove training ROI. 3. High employee turnover creates a continuous onboarding burden that consumes training team capacity. |
| **Metrics Owned** | Training completion rate, certification currency rate, time-to-productivity for new hires, training-to-performance correlation |
| **Buying Triggers** | 1. Compliance training audit or regulatory review finding gaps. 2. Leadership asking for training effectiveness data. |
| **Objections** | 1. "We already have an LMS — this duplicates what we have." 2. "Our content is already built in a different format." |
| **Relevant Modules** | LMS integration, Location Health Score (training dimension), Risk and Alerts Engine |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Connect training completions to location performance outcomes — see which training modules actually improve operational scores." |

## Persona 6: Compliance Manager

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | 1. Reduce audit failure rates and improve corrective action closure rates across the system. 2. Identify and address systemic compliance risk before it creates regulatory or brand exposure. 3. Shift from reactive compliance enforcement to proactive compliance risk management. |
| **Pain Points** | 1. Audit schedule is static — locations are audited on fixed cadences regardless of risk profile, meaning high-risk locations get the same attention as healthy ones. 2. Corrective action closure rates are chronically below 70% with no reliable mechanism to improve them. 3. Compliance risk is identified only during audits, not between them. |
| **Metrics Owned** | Audit pass rate, corrective action closure rate, repeat violation rate, days between audit and corrective action initiation |
| **Buying Triggers** | 1. Recent audit failure pattern or regulatory compliance notice. 2. Franchise system expansion creating more locations than current compliance team can audit on schedule. |
| **Objections** | 1. "Our franchisees resist additional compliance monitoring." 2. "We've had bad experiences with compliance tools that generated false positives." |
| **Relevant Modules** | Compliance and Audit Copilot, Closed-Loop Actions, Risk and Alerts Engine, Location Health Score |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Risk-stratified audit scheduling means you spend your compliance resources where the risk is highest — not where the calendar says." |

## Persona 7: Franchise Development Leader

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | 1. Accelerate new franchise sales without sacrificing franchisee quality. 2. Improve franchisee success prediction to reduce system-wide failure rates. 3. Demonstrate a compelling operational support story to franchise candidates. |
| **Pain Points** | 1. Franchisee success is difficult to predict at the selection stage — candidate quality assessment is subjective. 2. The transition from franchise development to franchise operations is poorly managed — context about new franchisees does not transfer. 3. Franchise development is competing against other investment opportunities that offer cleaner ROI stories. |
| **Metrics Owned** | New unit openings per year, franchisee success rate (Year 1-3), time-to-open, FDD Item 19 performance data |
| **Buying Triggers** | 1. Franchisee failure rate increasing, creating system-wide concern. 2. Franchise candidate due diligence requiring operational intelligence demonstration. |
| **Objections** | 1. "Our development and operations systems don't need to be integrated." 2. "Franchise candidates aren't asking about our operations technology." |
| **Relevant Modules** | CRM integration, Location Health Score, Pre-Opening Readiness |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Show franchise candidates exactly how your system supports their success — with data, not promises." |

## Persona 8: Franchisee / Multi-Unit Operator

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | 1. Run profitable, well-staffed, compliant locations without spending time on administrative burden. 2. Understand how their location(s) compare to peers and what the highest-leverage improvement actions are. 3. Have a productive, respectful relationship with the franchisor's operations team. |
| **Pain Points** | 1. HQ-imposed compliance requirements feel burdensome when the reasoning is not explained. 2. No access to peer benchmarking data — flying blind on how competitive their operational performance is. 3. Support from HQ is slow, inconsistent, and reactive — problems are escalated only after they are serious. |
| **Metrics Owned** | Location AUV, labor cost percentage, audit score, guest satisfaction score |
| **Buying Triggers** | 1. New location opening creating operational complexity. 2. Audit failure or compliance notice creating urgency. |
| **Objections** | 1. "Another platform means more for my staff to learn." 2. "HQ is just using this to monitor us, not help us." |
| **Relevant Modules** | Operator Command Center, Franchise Support Desk, Location Health Score (franchisee view) |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Know exactly where your locations stand — and exactly what actions will improve your performance — before HQ calls." |

## Persona 9: Store Manager

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|

| **Relevant Modules** | Franchise Support Desk (SOP assistant), Task Management, LMS |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Ask any operational question in plain language and get an instant answer sourced from your brand's own SOPs." |

## Persona 10: Frontline Employee

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|

| **Buying Triggers** | N/A — not a buyer; adoption is driven by manager and franchisee requirements |

| **Relevant Modules** | LMS (mobile training), Task Management, Franchise Support Desk |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Training that fits into your shift, not on top of it." |

## Persona 11: CFO / Finance Leader

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | 1. Maximize royalty collection efficiency and minimize royalty leakage. 2. Provide PE board and investors with location-level financial visibility and EBITDA attribution. 3. Reduce the manual labor required to produce financial reporting packages. |

| **Buying Triggers** | 1. PE board request for enhanced financial reporting. 2. Royalty delinquency incident triggering audit of reporting systems. |
| **Objections** | 1. "Financial systems belong in finance tools, not operations platforms." 2. "ROI on operations software is hard to quantify to my standards." |
| **Relevant Modules** | Executive Command Center (financial dimension), Data and Integrations Layer, Benchmarking |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Royalty anomaly detection and franchisee financial distress prediction before royalties go delinquent." |

## Persona 12: CMO / Marketing Leader

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | 1. Protect brand standards at the local marketing execution level. 2. Maximize the effectiveness of co-op marketing fund deployment. 3. Demonstrate the connection between marketing investment and location performance. |

| **Relevant Modules** | Location Health Score (customer experience dimension), Compliance Copilot (marketing standards), Benchmarking |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Customer experience signals from reviews and ratings — surfaced in the same platform as your operational compliance data." |

## Persona 13: PE Operating Partner

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | 1. Achieve the operational improvement targets in the PE investment thesis (EBITDA improvement, AUV growth, system-wide consistency). 2. Maintain portfolio-level visibility across all portfolio company locations. 3. Prepare portfolio companies for exit by building clean, demonstrable operational metrics. |
| **Pain Points** | 1. Portfolio-level operational visibility requires aggregating data from multiple portfolio companies using different tools and reporting formats. 2. Identifying underperforming assets early enough to intervene before they impact exit valuation. 3. Operational improvement initiatives across portfolio companies often lack measurement rigor — it is hard to know what is working. |
| **Metrics Owned** | Portfolio EBITDA trend, location health score distribution across portfolio, AUV variance, compliance risk concentration |
| **Buying Triggers** | 1. New portfolio company acquisition requiring operational technology assessment. 2. LP reporting cycle requiring enhanced operational metrics. 3. Portfolio company underperformance requiring operational intervention. |
| **Objections** | 1. "Portfolio companies have different systems and different needs — standardization is complex." 2. "We need to see ROI evidence before we invest across the portfolio." |
| **Relevant Modules** | Executive Command Center, Location Health Score, Risk and Alerts Engine, Benchmarking |
| **Messaging That Resonates** | "Portfolio-level operational intelligence for PE operating partners who need to see the full picture across every brand, every region, and every location — in one view." |
