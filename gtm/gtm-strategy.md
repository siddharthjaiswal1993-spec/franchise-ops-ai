# GTM Strategy: AI-Native Franchise Operational Intelligence

---

## Strategic GTM Thesis

The GTM strategy for AI-native franchise operational intelligence follows a deliberate land-and-expand motion. The entry point is a specific, high-pain workflow problem — field consultant operations — where AI delivers measurable ROI in weeks, not quarters. From that beachhead, the platform expands module by module, each expansion unlocking a new buyer persona, a new data source, and new revenue.

The sales motion is consultative and pilot-driven. Franchise operations leaders do not make $300,000-$1M+ annual software decisions based on feature demonstrations. They make them based on ROI evidence, peer references, and pilot outcomes. The GTM strategy is built to produce that evidence systematically.

## ICP Segments

### Primary ICP: Mid-Market Service-Heavy Franchise Brands (50-500 Locations)

**Profile:** National or regional franchise brands in QSR, fast casual, home services, fitness, education, or personal care verticals. Multi-state operations with a dedicated field consultant team (5-30 consultants). HQ operations team of 10-50 people. Royalty-based revenue model.

**Why they are the beachhead:** They are large enough that operational inconsistency has a measurable cost, but not so large that enterprise procurement complexity slows deal velocity. Field consultant teams are large enough to demonstrate AI ROI at scale. Decision-making is accessible at the VP or Director level. Contract value is $100,000-$500,000 annually.

### Secondary ICP 1: PE-Backed Franchise Brands (Any Size, 50-2000 Locations)

**Profile:** Franchise brands owned by or recently acquired by private equity firms. PE operating partners or portfolio company COOs are active technology buyers seeking portfolio-level intelligence, EBITDA improvement, and operational risk reduction.

**Why they are a priority:** PE-backed operators have defined financial performance mandates and active willingness to pay for tools that improve EBITDA visibility. They are also more likely to standardize technology across portfolio companies, creating multi-brand expansion opportunities.

### Secondary ICP 2: Multi-Unit Operators (5-100 Locations)

**Profile:** Franchisees managing multiple locations across one or more franchise systems. Professional management teams with back-office operations functions. Need portfolio visibility, labor cost benchmarking, and cross-location performance comparison.

**Why they matter:** MUOs are direct buyers (not just users). They control 40-60% of locations in major franchise systems. Platform adoption by MUOs creates bottom-up adoption pressure within franchise systems where FranConnect is not yet deployed at HQ.

### Secondary ICP 3: Enterprise Franchise Brands (500+ Locations)

**Profile:** National franchise brands with complex organizational structures, multiple business unit owners, and enterprise procurement requirements. Long sales cycles (12-18 months) but high contract values ($500K-$3M+ annually).

**Why they matter for platform credibility:** A handful of enterprise reference customers creates the brand authority needed for mid-market sales. Enterprise deployments also produce the highest volume of field visit and audit data for the AI models.

### Tertiary ICP: New Franchise Systems (20-50 Locations, Rapid Growth)

**Profile:** Emerging franchise brands that have proven their model but are scaling fast enough that manual operations management is breaking. Founder-led or early operations leadership team. Budget-constrained but growth-motivated.

**Why they matter:** High growth rate means these brands become mid-market ICP in 24-36 months. Winning them early locks in platform embeddedness before they are large enough for competitive displacement.

## Beachhead Recommendation

The recommended beachhead is **mid-market service-heavy franchise brands in QSR, home services, or fitness verticals with 50-500 locations and a dedicated field consultant team.**

These brands share a common set of conditions that make the AI Field Consultant wedge immediately valuable:
- Field consultants are a significant operational cost (5-30 FTEs, $600K-$4M annually)
- Visit quality is inconsistent and unmeasured
- Corrective action closure rates are below 65%
- POS and audit data exist but are not connected
- Leadership is already aware that field consulting is under-tooled

The beachhead is tight enough to generate focused case study evidence, loose enough to provide sufficient pipeline volume.

## The Wedge: AI Field Consultant

The AI Field Consultant module is the primary wedge for three reasons:

**First, it attacks the highest-pain problem.** Field consultants are the highest-cost operational asset in franchise systems that are currently operating with inadequate tools. VP-level buyers feel this acutely and have the budget authority to address it.

**Second, it creates measurable pilot ROI.** A pilot with 10 field consultants across 30 locations over 8 weeks produces specific, measurable outcomes: hours of administrative time recaptured per consultant per week; improvement in corrective action closure rates; improvement in visit quality scores; reduction in time-to-visit-summary submission. These are numbers the sales team can use.

**Third, it creates data exhaust for everything else.** Every AI-assisted field visit generates structured, tagged operational data. This is the raw material for the Location Health Score, the Compliance Copilot, and the Executive Intelligence Layer. The wedge does not just solve the field consulting problem — it builds the data infrastructure for the platform.

### Pilot Design

A standard AI Field Consultant pilot runs 8 weeks with the following structure:
- 10 field consultants from the same brand
- 30 assigned locations (3 per consultant)
- Week 1-2: Onboarding, integration setup, baseline metric capture
- Week 3-6: Live AI-assisted visits with pre-visit briefs, voice notes, and corrective action auto-drafting
- Week 7-8: Data analysis, ROI calculation, case study documentation

Target ROI metrics:
- 8+ hours/week recaptured per field consultant (currently spent on administrative tasks)
- 25%+ improvement in corrective action closure rate
- 40%+ reduction in time from visit to visit summary submission
- 90%+ field consultant satisfaction with pre-visit briefs

## Sales Motion: Consultative, Demo-Led, Pilot-First, Land-and-Expand

**Consultative approach:** The sale begins with operational diagnosis. The sales team uses a structured discovery framework to quantify the buyer's current operational cost — field consultant admin burden, corrective action closure rates, audit failure rates, visit frequency vs. risk distribution. This positions the sale as operational improvement rather than software purchasing.

**Demo design:** The demo follows the DETECT -> DECIDE -> ACT -> VERIFY loop, starting with a location health score alert and walking through the full cycle to verified outcome. The demo script is built around the specific operational pain points surfaced in discovery.

**Pilot-first close:** Rather than closing a full platform sale in the first motion, the sales team closes a funded 8-week AI Field Consultant pilot at a defined price point ($15,000-$25,000). The pilot converts to a full platform contract if ROI targets are met.

**Land-and-expand triggers:**
- Pilot completion and ROI validation → full field consultant platform contract
- Field consultant platform + 6-month track record → Location Health Score module expansion
- Health score deployment → Compliance Copilot expansion
- Multi-unit operator adoption → Operator Command Center expansion
- Senior leadership engagement → Executive Intelligence Layer

## Pricing Principles

The platform is priced per location per month, with an AI intelligence tier premium over the base workflow tier. Per-user pricing is explicitly avoided — in franchise operations, the economic value is in the number of locations managed, not the number of HQ users.

## Channel Strategy

**Direct sales:** Primary channel for mid-market and enterprise ICP. Inside sales for under-100-location prospects; field sales for 100+ location prospects and PE-backed brands.

**Franchise industry events:** IFA Annual Convention (February), Multi-Unit Franchising Conference (March), Franchise Operations Executive Forum. Conference-based pipeline sourcing and thought leadership presentation.

**Franchise consultant channel:** 200+ franchise consultants and brokers recommend technology platforms to their franchisor clients. A structured partner program with referral economics (10-15% first-year referral fee) and co-selling support accelerates mid-market coverage.

**PE operating partner channel:** Direct outreach to PE firms with franchise portfolio exposure (Consumer + QSR PE funds). Relationship-driven, invitation-only roundtable program featuring portfolio operational intelligence benchmarking. See `gtm/partnership-strategy.md` for full detail.

**System integrators:** For enterprise deployments requiring custom integration and change management, certified SI partners extend sales capacity and implementation credibility.
