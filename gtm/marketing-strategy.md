# Marketing Strategy: AI-Native Franchise Operational Intelligence

---

## Strategic Narrative and Positioning

The marketing strategy is organized around a single strategic narrative: **franchise operations is moving from systems of record to systems of intelligence, and the brands that make this transition will outperform those that don't.**

Every content asset, every campaign, and every customer touchpoint should advance this narrative. The goal is not to market features. It is to position FranConnect as the company that understands where franchise operations is going — and has built the platform to get there.

## Demand Generation Playbook

### Asset 1: Annual Franchise Operations Intelligence Benchmark Report

**What it is:** An annual research report based on aggregated platform data (de-identified) and a survey of 300+ franchise operators. Covers average audit scores, corrective action closure rates, training completion rates, field consultant productivity, and location health score distribution by vertical.

**Why it works:** Benchmark data is intrinsically shareable — every operator wants to know where they stand relative to their peers. The report creates organic distribution through franchise associations, consultant networks, and social sharing. It positions FranConnect as the source of operational truth in franchise operations.

**Production:** Annual, with quarterly update emails to subscribers. Gated download generates pipeline; ungated summary distributed widely for awareness.

### Asset 2: Franchise Operational Maturity Assessment

**What it is:** A 15-minute interactive self-assessment that helps franchise operators identify where they are on the systems-of-record to systems-of-intelligence maturity curve. Produces a personalized maturity score, a comparison to industry benchmarks, and a prioritized recommendation set.

**Why it works:** Maturity assessments are high-intent conversion tools. A VP of Operations who completes the assessment is in active evaluation mode. The personalized output creates immediate, relevant follow-up opportunities for the sales team.

**Distribution:** Paid LinkedIn, IFA conference, email nurture, website homepage.

### Asset 3: Field Consultant ROI Calculator

**What it is:** An interactive calculator that takes field consultant team size and current visit workflow inputs and calculates the hours of administrative time recaptured, the improvement in corrective action closure rates, and the estimated annual cost savings from AI-assisted field operations.

**Why it works:** ROI calculators give VP of Operations buyers a business case they can take to the COO or CFO. The calculator output is a shareable PDF — creating a ready-made internal selling tool.

### Asset 4: Webinar Series — "Franchise Intelligence Quarterly"

**What it is:** A quarterly webinar series featuring FranConnect leaders, franchise operations experts, and guest customers. Topics aligned with the 10 content themes.

**Format:** 45 minutes with 15-minute Q&A. Recorded and distributed as on-demand content. Transcribed for content repurposing.

**Distribution:** Email to existing customer base; LinkedIn paid promotion to target personas; partner co-promotion with franchise consultants and associations.

### Asset 5: Case Study Library

**What it is:** A library of detailed customer case studies organized by vertical, company size, and use case. Each case study includes pre-engagement situation, problem statement, solution deployed, and quantified outcomes (hours saved, audit score improvement, corrective action closure rate improvement).

**Standard:** Minimum 3 quantified metrics per case study. Video version for highest-performing case studies.

## Campaign Concepts

### Campaign 1: "The 35% Problem" — Field Consultant Productivity

**Premise:** Field consultants spend 35% of their time on administrative tasks. This campaign quantifies the cost of that problem and presents the AI Field Consultant as the solution.

**Channels:** LinkedIn sponsored content, email to VP Operations and Director of Field Operations personas, conference collateral at MUFC and IFA.

**Content:** Infographic (35% stat), short video testimonial from a field consultant, landing page with ROI calculator, case study download.

**Conversion goal:** Maturity assessment completion or AI Field Consultant demo request.

### Campaign 2: "Portfolio Health Check" — PE and MUO Segment

**Premise:** PE operating partners and MUOs don't have a single view of portfolio health. This campaign presents the Executive Intelligence Layer and Operator Command Center as the solution.

**Channels:** LinkedIn targeting PE operating partners and franchise executives, PE industry newsletter advertising, roundtable event invitations.

**Content:** Executive white paper ("Operational Intelligence for PE-Backed Franchise Brands"), roundtable event invitation, demo video of Executive Command Center.

### Campaign 3: "Corrective Action Graveyard" — Compliance Leaders

**Premise:** Most corrective actions are never formally closed. This campaign targets Compliance Managers with a quantified problem statement and the Compliance Copilot solution.

**Channels:** LinkedIn, IFA Franchise Legal Symposium, email to compliance persona list.

**Content:** Infographic (corrective action closure rate benchmark), white paper on closed-loop compliance management, demo video.

### Campaign 4: Annual Benchmark Report Launch

**Premise:** The annual benchmark report is a flagship demand generation asset. Its launch is an event — distributed across all channels, promoted at IFA, and treated as thought leadership positioning for the full year.

**Channels:** PR outreach to franchise trade media (Franchise Times, Franchise Update, Nation's Restaurant News), LinkedIn, email, IFA distribution.

### Campaign 5: "Before the Report Tells You It's Too Late"

**Premise:** Operational reports show what happened. This campaign challenges franchise operators who rely on reporting to think about what they are missing.

**Channels:** LinkedIn, trade media advertising, direct mail to VP Operations at mid-market franchise brands.

**Content:** 30-second video ad (executive reviewing report; cut to a location having problems no one knows about yet), case study showing early detection value.

### Campaign 6: Franchisee-Facing Campaign — "Know Your Score Before HQ Does"

**Premise:** Franchisees and MUOs want to know how their locations are performing before field consultants or HQ identifies a problem. This campaign targets the franchisee and MUO persona directly.

**Channels:** Franchise association publications, franchisee councils, direct email to franchise owners.

## Event Marketing Strategy

### IFA Annual Convention (February)
**Priority:** Highest. The IFA convention is the single most concentrated gathering of franchise operations buyers in the industry.
**Strategy:** Speaking session (category narrative: systems of record to intelligence), executive roundtable (PE operating partners and COOs), booth with Location Health Score live demo, pre-event benchmark report distribution.

### Multi-Unit Franchising Conference (March)
**Priority:** High. MUOs are a priority ICP and this event is 100% MUO audience.
**Strategy:** Sponsored breakfast session, MUO-specific messaging for Operator Command Center, case study material from MUO customers.

### Franchise Operations Executive Forum
**Strategy:** Position as thought leadership event, not sales event. Invite senior operations leaders to a structured operational intelligence discussion.

### Franchisee Association Meetings
**Strategy:** Direct outreach to regional franchisee associations for speaking opportunities. Franchisee council endorsement creates bottom-up HQ pressure.

## PE Operating Partner Roundtable Program

**Concept:** An invitation-only annual roundtable for PE operating partners with franchise portfolio exposure. Hosted by FranConnect leadership, featuring de-identified portfolio benchmarking data, operational intelligence case studies, and facilitated peer discussion.

**Why it works:** PE operating partners have significant buying influence but are not accessible through conventional demand generation. An executive roundtable program that delivers genuine peer intelligence creates the relationship entry point for multi-portfolio deployments.

**Format:** Half-day in-person event in a financial center city (New York, Chicago, Dallas); 15-20 invitees; no product sales pitch; follow-up one-on-one meetings with interested participants.
