# Partnership Strategy: Franchise Operational Intelligence Platform

---

## Partnership Philosophy

The partnership strategy is designed to extend market reach, accelerate pipeline generation, deepen integration value, and strengthen the intelligence platform's data moat — without diluting product focus or creating channel conflict.

Every partnership must pass a three-part test: Does it bring us closer to the right buyer? Does it make the platform more valuable to existing customers? Does it create data or distribution advantages that compound over time?

---

## Partner Category 1: Franchise Consultant and Broker Channel

### Who They Are

Franchise consultants and brokers — often called franchise advisors or franchise development consultants — help prospective franchisors and franchisees evaluate franchise opportunities, develop franchise systems, and navigate franchise operations decisions. There are estimated 300-500 active franchise consultants in the U.S. through networks like Fransmart, FranChoice, and FranNet, plus independent practitioners.

### Why They Matter

Franchise consultants are trusted advisors to the franchise systems that are making technology decisions. When a new franchisor is building their operations technology stack, or an existing franchisor is evaluating a platform change, consultants are often the first call. A recommendation from a trusted consultant can accelerate a deal from cold outreach to active evaluation in weeks.

### Partnership Structure

- **Referral fee:** 10-15% of first-year contract value for qualified introductions that convert to signed contracts
- **Training:** Annual certification program — 2-hour online training + assessment — qualifying consultants to recommend and discuss the platform
- **Co-marketing:** Joint webinars with consultant partners, co-branded content, shared presence at IFA and regional events
- **Partner portal:** Deal registration, co-selling resources, and marketing collateral access
- **Target:** 50 active certified partners in Year 1; 150 by Year 3

### Activation Plan

Month 1-2: Identify top 50 franchise consultants with QSR and home services franchise focus. Outreach from VP Sales with personal invitation to partner program.
Month 3-4: Launch partner certification program. First joint webinar with 3-5 partner consultants.
Month 5-12: Activate referral economics. Measure pipeline generated per partner.

## Partner Category 2: Franchisor Association Partnerships

### Who They Are

The International Franchise Association (IFA), regional franchise associations (FranPAC, state-level associations), and vertical franchise associations (the Franchise Finance and Development Association, etc.).

### Why They Matter

Association partnerships provide access to the entire franchise industry through a single trusted channel. IFA membership includes over 3,000 franchise brands. Endorsed vendor status or preferred partner designation from IFA carries significant credibility signal to mid-market and enterprise buyers.

### Partnership Structure

- **IFA preferred partner designation:** Annual sponsorship investment; inclusion in vendor directory; speaking opportunities at IFA Annual Convention
- **Content partnerships:** Co-branded research reports (IFA + FranConnect franchise operations benchmark data); joint webinars distributed to IFA membership
- **Educational content:** Contribute to IFA's franchise executive education programs and training curricula
- **Regional association outreach:** Smaller investment but access to concentrated regional buyer networks

### Activation Plan

Engage IFA Government Relations and Partnership team for preferred vendor evaluation. Prepare co-branded benchmark report proposal for IFA review. Secure speaking slot at next IFA Annual Convention.

## Partner Category 3: POS Integration Partnerships

### Who They Are

Point-of-sale system providers serving the franchise verticals where the platform operates: Toast (dominant in restaurant), Square, PAR Technology, Revel Systems, Aloha (NCR), Oracle MICROS, and Lightspeed.

### Why They Matter

POS data is the single most valuable data source for the Location Health Score. Sales trends, transaction counts, average ticket, and labor cost data from POS systems are the foundation of financial intelligence in the health model. The quality and depth of POS integrations directly determines the quality of financial dimension scoring in the location health model.

POS partnerships also create co-selling opportunities. Toast's franchise-focused sales team is actively selling to QSR franchisors who are also FranConnect ICP. A formal partnership with joint sales motion and co-marketing can source qualified pipeline from both sides.

### Partnership Structure

- **Technical integration:** Certified API integration with major POS systems; real-time webhook support for event-driven data ingestion; listed in POS vendor marketplace or app store
- **Co-selling agreements:** Joint lead sharing for franchise customers using both platforms; co-branded ROI case studies
- **Product positioning:** FranConnect listed as recommended franchise operations platform for franchisors using the POS system

### Priority POS Partners

1. **Toast** (highest priority — dominant in QSR franchise, active franchise-focused GTM)
2. **PAR Technology** (strong QSR enterprise presence)
3. **Square for Restaurants** (growing franchise customer base)
4. **Oracle MICROS** (enterprise restaurant and hospitality)

## Partner Category 4: Payroll and HRIS Integrations

### Who They Are

Payroll and HR platform providers serving multi-location businesses: ADP (dominant mid-market and enterprise), Paychex (strong mid-market), Rippling (growing SMB and mid-market), Workday (enterprise).

### Why They Matter

Labor cost and turnover data from payroll and HRIS systems are critical inputs for both the Location Health Score (financial dimension, staffing stability dimension) and the AI Field Consultant (pre-visit brief includes recent staff changes and certification lapses).

A franchise location that has had three manager changes in 90 days is a risk signal. A franchise location with labor cost above brand average by 8 percentage points is a financial risk signal. These signals require payroll integration to surface.

### Partnership Structure

- **Technical integration:** API-based employee data sync, labor cost data ingestion, turnover rate calculation
- **Marketplace listing:** Listed in ADP and Paychex partner marketplace directories
- **Co-marketing:** Joint content for mid-market franchise audiences on workforce management and operational intelligence

### Priority Payroll/HRIS Partners

1. **ADP** (highest franchise penetration; strong mid-market and enterprise)
2. **Paychex** (strong mid-market; accessible for mid-tier franchise brands)
3. **Rippling** (fast-growing; strong for technology-forward franchise brands)

## Partner Category 5: PE Operating Partner Channel

### Who They Are

Private equity firms with franchise portfolio exposure, their operating partner teams, and specialist franchise-focused PE advisors.

### Why They Matter

PE operating partners have multi-portfolio buying authority. A single relationship with a PE operating partner at a firm with 5 franchise portfolio companies is a path to 5 simultaneous platform evaluations — an extraordinary pipeline efficiency multiplier.

PE operating partners are also influential reference voices. When a PE-backed franchisor evaluates technology, the PE operating partner's opinion carries weight. Building relationships at the PE firm level creates influence at the portfolio company level.

### Partnership Structure

- **Roundtable program:** Annual invitation-only PE operating partner roundtable (see Marketing Strategy for full design)
- **Portfolio benchmark service:** Annual operational benchmark analysis for PE operating partners who deploy the platform across portfolio companies — de-identified cross-portfolio benchmarking delivered as an executive service
- **Co-investment relationships:** Where PE firms are evaluating franchise software investments, offer strategic advisory relationships that build familiarity with platform capabilities
- **Referral economics:** Portfolio company referrals to FranConnect resulting in signed contracts earn a partnership benefit (access to cross-portfolio benchmarking data, not cash referral fees, to avoid compliance complexity)

### Target PE Firms

Franchise-focused consumer PE firms; mid-market PE with QSR and home services exposure; PE-backed franchise management companies. Research priority: identify operating partners at 20 target firms.

## Partner Category 6: System Integrator Partnerships for Enterprise Deployments

### Who They Are

Technology consultants and system integrators who advise and implement technology decisions for enterprise franchise brands: Deloitte Digital, Accenture (Operations practice), specialized franchise technology consultants.

### Why They Matter

Enterprise franchise brands (500+ locations) frequently engage system integrators to manage complex technology implementations, integration architecture, and change management. An SI recommendation or inclusion in an SI's franchise technology stack recommendation gives FranConnect enterprise sales credibility and implementation support.

### Partnership Structure

- **Certification program:** SI partner training on FranConnect platform architecture and implementation methodology
- **Deal support:** Co-selling with SI partners in enterprise opportunities; FranConnect provides technical resources, SI provides implementation capacity
- **Referral economics:** Implementation work flows to SI; FranConnect software revenue stays with FranConnect; SI earns margin on services
- **Pipeline sharing:** SIs bring enterprise franchise clients into FranConnect evaluation; FranConnect brings enterprise client opportunities to SI delivery partners

### Target SI Partners

Deloitte's franchise practice; boutique franchise technology consultants; regional management consultants with franchise sector focus.
