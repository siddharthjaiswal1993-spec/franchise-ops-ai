# Pricing and Packaging: Franchise Operational Intelligence Platform

---

## Pricing Philosophy

The pricing architecture for a franchise operational intelligence platform must align with how value is created and perceived by franchise buyers. Four principles drive the packaging design:

**Principle 1: Per-location pricing, not per-user.** In franchise operations, the value of the platform scales with the number of locations managed — not the number of HQ users. A franchisor managing 200 locations has 200x the operational risk surface area of a franchisor managing 1 location. Pricing per location creates alignment between platform value and platform revenue.

**Principle 2: Unlimited frontline users within the per-location fee.** Franchisors should not be penalized for employee adoption. If a 30-employee location generates 30 active users, that should not create incremental cost. Unlimited frontline users within the per-location fee drives maximum adoption and maximum data generation, which feeds the AI intelligence layer.

**Principle 3: AI intelligence as a premium tier.** The workflow foundation (task management, basic LMS, audit forms, basic field visits) is the entry-level product. The AI intelligence layer (Location Health Score, predictive alerts, AI-generated corrective action plans, executive intelligence briefs) commands a meaningful premium because it delivers measurably different ROI.

**Principle 4: Expansion economics through module adoption, not seat expansion.** Revenue expansion comes from moving existing customers from Foundation to Intelligence tiers (3-5x revenue per location), and from expanding the location count as franchise systems grow — not from seat-count expansion.

## Package Structure

### Foundation Tier — Workflow and Operations
**Price range:** $8-15 per location per month

**Included capabilities:**
- Basic franchise CRM and development pipeline
- Franchisee onboarding and pre-opening checklists
- Core LMS (training module delivery, completion tracking)
- Digital audit forms with basic scoring
- Task management and mobile checklist execution
- Basic corrective action creation and tracking (manual)
- Royalty calculation and invoicing
- Standard reporting and dashboards

**Target buyer:** Emerging franchise systems (25-100 locations) evaluating their first professional operations platform; brands currently on point solutions looking to consolidate.

**Value proposition:** "Replace your spreadsheets and binders with a single platform your whole team can use."

---

### Operations Tier — Field and Compliance
**Price range:** $20-35 per location per month

**Includes Foundation, plus:**
- Advanced field consultant workspace (visit scheduling, structured visit forms, visit history)
- Visit-linked corrective action management with deadline tracking and escalation
- Advanced LMS (role-based curricula, certification tracking, compliance training)
- Compliance-focused audit capabilities (multi-category scoring, scheduled and surprise audits)
- Franchisee communication portal with structured messaging
- Basic operational reporting with field team performance dashboards
- Integration connectors for 2-3 core systems (POS, payroll)

**Target buyer:** Mid-market franchise brands (50-300 locations) with a field consulting team that needs structure and measurement; brands ready to move beyond basic workflow digitization.

**Value proposition:** "Give your field team the tools and structure to drive consistent operational improvement — and measure whether it's working."

---

### Intelligence Tier — AI-Native Operational Intelligence
**Price range:** $45-80 per location per month

**Includes Operations Tier, plus:**
- Location Health Score (continuously updated, explainable, benchmarked)
- AI Field Consultant (pre-visit brief generation, voice-to-structured notes, auto-corrective action drafting)
- AI-powered risk and alerts engine (proactive anomaly detection, score state notifications)
- Predictive compliance risk scoring and risk-stratified audit scheduling
- AI-generated corrective action plan recommendations with source citations and confidence scoring
- Operator Command Center (multi-unit operator portfolio view)
- Benchmarking engine (peer group, brand average, top-quartile comparisons)
- Full integration ecosystem (POS, LMS, HRIS, payroll, review platforms, ticketing)
- 60-day outcome verification reporting

**Target buyer:** Mid-market and enterprise franchise brands (100+ locations) with active field consulting teams; PE-backed franchisors; brands where operational inconsistency has a quantifiable business cost.

**Value proposition:** "Detect operational risk before it shows up in your reports. Know what to do. Track whether it worked."

---

### Enterprise Intelligence Tier — Autonomous Operations and Executive Layer
**Price range:** $80-150+ per location per month (custom pricing for large accounts)

**Includes Intelligence Tier, plus:**
- Executive Command Center (portfolio-level health, board-ready reports, AI executive briefs)
- PE Operating Partner dashboard (cross-portfolio intelligence, LP reporting metrics)
- AI Agent access (AI Launch Coordinator, AI Compliance Agent, AI Financial Analyst in supervised mode)
- Cross-brand benchmarking (compare against all brands in the same vertical on the platform)
- Outcome-linked AI reporting (intervention efficacy analysis, model performance metrics)
- Dedicated success manager and quarterly business reviews
- Custom integration development for enterprise systems (SAP, Oracle, Workday)
- SLA commitments with uptime guarantees and support response times

**Target buyer:** Enterprise franchise brands (500+ locations), PE operating partners overseeing multi-brand portfolios, brands preparing for PE exit requiring clean operational metrics.

**Value proposition:** "Portfolio-level operational intelligence that gives your CEO and board a real-time picture of system health — with AI that surfaces what matters and verifies that interventions worked."

## Expansion Economics: A Worked Example

**Starting scenario:** A mid-market QSR franchise brand with 120 locations currently on the Operations Tier at $28/location/month.

**Year 0 (contract):** $28 x 120 locations x 12 months = **$40,320 ARR**

**Months 1-6:** AI Field Consultant pilot launches for 10 FCs covering 30 locations. ROI demonstrated: 9 hours/week recaptured per FC, corrective action closure rate up from 52% to 74%, average audit score up 6 points at pilot locations.

**Month 7:** Full AI Field Consultant module expanded to all 120 locations. Customer upgrades to Intelligence Tier at $58/location/month. **ARR increases to $83,520** (+107%)

**Month 12:** Operator Command Center adopted by 8 MUOs in the system (each managing 5-15 locations). Additional MUO revenue added.

**Month 15:** Brand adds 20 new locations (franchise system growth). **ARR increases to $95,760** (new location expansion at Intelligence Tier rate)

**Month 18:** Executive Intelligence Layer added for CEO and PE Operating Partner. Enterprise tier upgrade for HQ users. Custom pricing negotiated: **Estimated ARR $115,000-$135,000**

**24-month NRR: 285-335%** from a single brand relationship.

This expansion path — from workflow foundation to full intelligence tier to enterprise layer — is the defining economic driver of the platform's growth. It is driven by demonstrated ROI at each stage, not by upsell pressure.

## Why Per-User Pricing Is Wrong for This Market

Per-user pricing creates three structural problems in franchise operations:

**Problem 1: It penalizes franchisee and frontline adoption.** A 30-employee location with 30 active users should not cost 30x a 3-user HQ admin location. Yet that is the outcome of per-user pricing. The result is that franchisors restrict user licenses to reduce cost, which reduces adoption, which reduces the data quality that powers the AI intelligence layer.

**Problem 2: It misaligns with operational value.** The operational value of a franchise software platform scales with the number of locations managed, not the number of admin users. A brand with 500 locations and 20 HQ users has 25x the operational complexity of a brand with 20 locations — but per-user pricing would charge them 1x.

**Problem 3: It creates the wrong expansion metric.** Per-user pricing incentivizes the vendor to count headcount growth, not location growth or module adoption. The result is a pricing model that focuses vendor sales energy on adding users rather than demonstrating operational intelligence value.

Per-location pricing with unlimited users solves all three problems: it aligns revenue with operational scale, enables maximum frontline adoption, and focuses expansion on location growth and intelligence tier upgrades.

| Zenput/Crunchtime | $15-35/location/month | Per-location | Compliance-focused |
| Target Intelligence Tier | $45-80/location/month | Per-location + tier | Premium justified by AI intelligence ROI |

The intelligence tier premium is justified by the ROI differential: a workflow platform that saves an operations manager 2 hours per week is worth $10-20/location/month. An intelligence platform that prevents a location from deteriorating from At Risk to Critical — a cost savings of $25,000-$75,000 in rescue intervention — is worth $45-80/location/month.

The ROI conversation must be part of every intelligence tier sale.
