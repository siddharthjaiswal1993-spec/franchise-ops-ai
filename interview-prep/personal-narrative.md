# Personal Narrative: Preparing for FranConnect

---

## Why Franchise Operations

My interest in franchise operations did not start with a franchise brand or a software company. It started with a question I found myself asking repeatedly across different roles: why is operational consistency so hard to maintain at scale?

In product and GTM work across B2B software, I kept encountering the same fundamental problem from different angles. Organizations build great processes, document them carefully, train their teams — and then watch execution diverge as soon as it gets distributed across multiple locations, regions, or teams. The documentation is there. The training was done. But reality at the point of execution is different from the intent at the point of design.

Franchise operations is where this problem is most acute, most expensive, and most structurally interesting. A franchise brand has independently owned locations, each with its own owner, staff, and local context. The brand promises consistency. The reality is distributed execution. The gap between those two things is where hundreds of millions of dollars of value are either created or destroyed every year.

---

## The Research and the Prototype

When I started researching the franchise software market, I expected to find that the problem was mostly solved. It was not. I found an industry running on surprisingly outdated operational infrastructure — spreadsheets for field consultant visit planning, email for corrective action tracking, quarterly reports for risk management. The technology landscape was more fragmented and more reactive than the scale of the problem warranted.

I also found a clear pattern in the competitive landscape: the incumbent platforms had solved the workflow digitization problem but had not yet built the operational intelligence layer. And the mobile-first challengers had excellent frontline UX but lacked the franchise-native depth and intelligence architecture to serve the VP-and-above buyer.

That gap — between what franchise operations leaders actually need and what the available software provides — is where I focused my research.

The research paper I wrote ("Beyond Franchise Management Software") and the prototype I built (https://nexus-foresight-hub.lovable.app) were both attempts to articulate what a genuinely AI-native franchise operations platform would look like — one built around the DETECT -> DECIDE -> ACT -> VERIFY loop, with the AI Field Consultant as the primary wedge and the Location Health Score as the unifying intelligence asset.
